Origins of the NY Philharmonic

The Origins of the New York Philharmonic seeks to analyze the influence that 5 lead conductors― Carl Bergmann, Leopold Damrosch, Theodore Thomas, Walter Damrosch, and Gustav Mahler had on the development of the early New York Philharmonic. Using the data from New York Philharmonic’s Performance History database, the project analyzes conductor influence on the growth of a new music community in the U.S., and the trajectory of the New York Philharmonic from 1842-1911. The project utilizes data extracted from The New York Philharmonic Leon Levy Digital Archives, which was used to examine popularity trends amongst leading conductors. The Origins of the New York Philharmonic project aims to connect personal histories and narratives to the roots and founding of the prestigious New York Philharmonic. NY Philharmonic History

Founded in 1842, the New York Philharmonic is the oldest symphonic orchestra to operate out of the United States. Since conception, the Philharmonic has commissioned and debuted works by leading composers, such as Dvořák, Rachmaninoff, Beethoven, and Brahms.

German Influence

The formative years of the New York Philharmonic are defined primarily by the success and tribulations of its early conductors. The groundwork for what would later become the New York Philharmonic was laid alongside the rise of German immigration in the 19th century.
